Mailbox Repainting & Refurbishing Procedure

1. Prep Work

  1. Inspect the mailbox: Check for rust, dents, or damage.
  2. Remove the mailbox (if needed): Use a screwdriver for easier access to all surfaces.
  3. Clean thoroughly: Mix water with mild soap and scrub down the mailbox and post. Rinse and dry completely.

2. Sanding

  1. Use a wire brush to remove rust spots or loose paint.
  2. Follow with sandpaper (start with medium grit, then fine) for a smooth surface.
  3. Wipe down the dust with a dry rag.

3. Taping and Covering

  1. Use painter’s tape to protect address numbers, flag, and any parts you don’t want painted.
  2. Lay down a drop cloth to keep paint off surrounding areas.

4. Priming

  1. Apply a rust-preventive primer spray in thin, even coats.
  2. Allow it to dry completely (15–30 minutes).

5. Painting

  1. Shake spray paint well and apply 2–3 thin coats. Hold the can about 6–10 inches away.
  2. Let each coat dry for 15–30 minutes before the next.
  3. Optional: Add a clear coat spray for a glossy finish and durability.

6. Custom Design

  1. Once the paint is dry, apply vinyl decals or use stencils for house numbers or designs.
  2. Press decals down firmly and smooth out air bubbles.

7. Reinstall & Clean Up

  1. Reattach the mailbox (if removed).
  2. Remove all tape and plastic sheeting.
  3. Take before-and-after photos for your portfolio!

Deluxe Procedure

  1. Prepare the Ground: Shape flower bed, add soil, level surface leaving space for rocks.
  2. Lay Landscape Fabric: Cut and lay over the soil; secure with staples or soil.
  3. Add Decorative Rocks: Spread rocks evenly without burying plant stems.
  4. Final Touches: Brush off rocks/dirt and center everything for a clean finish.
